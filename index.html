The case

What happened

  1. $3,000 paid in full, up front

    Full payment was made before any work began, for the agreed remodeling job.

  2. The work was never started

    After taking payment, the contractor never began the job — no materials, no crew, no show.

  3. No refund has been given

    Despite repeated requests, none of the $3,000 has been returned.

  4. Phone calls go unanswered

    Calls are not returned. The only responses ever received came by text message.

  5. Repeated promises, never kept

    Texts promised a refund or a start date more than once. Neither ever happened.

If you’re about to hire a contractor

How to protect yourself

  • Verify the license and insurance with Georgia’s licensing board before paying a cent.
  • Never pay in full up front — tie payments to completed milestones in writing.
  • Get a signed contract covering full scope, price, and timeline.
  • Keep everything in writing, and save all receipts and messages.
Report & resources

Where to take action

If you’ve had a similar experience, these are the places worth contacting:

Georgia Licensing Board →
Often the most effective lever against a contractor. File a complaint with the state.
Small Claims (Magistrate Court) →
In Georgia you can file claims up to $15,000 yourself, no lawyer needed. Find your county’s court.
Better Business Bureau →
File a formal, public complaint against the business.
FTC — Report Fraud →
Reports build a federal record, though they don’t resolve a case directly.
